In this guide: Legal methods to save YouTube content for offline viewing — YouTube Premium, YouTube Go, and understanding copyright rules for educational use.

Legal Methods to Download YouTube

  1. YouTube Premium: ₹139/month — official offline download for mobile
  2. YouTube Studio: Download your own uploaded videos
  3. YouTube Go app: Share videos offline in same WiFi
  4. Screen recording: Legal for personal use in most jurisdictions

Copyright Rules

Most YouTube content is copyright protected. Downloading without permission violates YouTube ToS and copyright law. Exceptions: Creative Commons licensed videos, Public Domain content, content you own.

Fair Use Guidelines

Educational use, commentary, criticism, and news reporting may qualify as fair use — but this does not make downloading legal. Always check the video license in YouTube description.

Safe Alternatives

  • Save videos to Watch Later playlist
  • Use YouTube Premium for legal offline viewing
  • For education: use YouTube's clip feature or embed
  • Contact creators for permission to download
