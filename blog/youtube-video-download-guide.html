Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is it illegal to download YouTube videos?

A: For personal offline viewing and educational use, it's generally acceptable. However, redistributing, re-uploading, or commercial use without permission is illegal.

Q: Can YouTube detect if I download videos?

A: YouTube cannot directly detect downloads using third-party tools. However, it's against their Terms of Service unless you have explicit permission.

Q: Which format should I download?

A: MP4 is the most universal format, compatible with all devices and media players.

Q: Can I download copyrighted content?

A: Technically yes, but using it for anything beyond personal viewing is illegal. Better to stick with Creative Commons or your own uploaded content.

Q: Does downloading hurt creators?

A: It can reduce ad revenue. Support creators by watching online when possible, or consider YouTube Premium which shares subscription revenue.

Conclusion

Downloading YouTube videos can be convenient for offline viewing, but it's important to use this capability responsibly. Always respect copyright laws, support content creators, and use downloads only for personal, educational purposes.
