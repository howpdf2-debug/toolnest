What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice is a legal document showing goods/services sold with GST amount charged. Every registered Indian business must issue tax invoices for taxable supplies.

Key Components of a GST Invoice

  • Invoice Number: Unique sequential number (e.g., INV-001)
  • Invoice Date: Date of supply
  • Supplier Details: Name, address, and GSTIN
  • Customer Details: Name, address, and GSTIN (for B2B)
  • Item Details: Description, quantity, unit price
  • Tax Breakdown: CGST + SGST (intra-state) or IGST (inter-state)
  • Grand Total: Final amount payable

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Missing GSTIN: Include valid GSTIN in every invoice
  2. Wrong tax type: IGST inter-state, CGST+SGST intra-state
  3. Duplicate invoice numbers: Always sequential, always unique
  4. Incorrect calculations: Use automated tools
  5. Missing due date: Always specify payment terms

GST Invoice Rules 2026

Key regulations:
  • Mandatory for taxable supplies above ₹200
  • Issue within 30 days of supply
  • Digital invoices legally valid in India
  • E-invoicing mandatory for turnover above ₹5 crore
  • Preserve invoices for 72 months (6 years)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online invoice legally valid?

Yes. Digital invoices with all mandatory fields are legally valid in India and accepted for GST compliance.

Do I need GST registration?

If annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special states), registration is mandatory.

Can I edit after issuing?

No. Issue a credit/debit note, or cancel and create a new invoice with a different number.

GST formula?

GST Amount = (Base × Rate) ÷ 100. Example: 18% on ₹1,000 = ₹180. Total = ₹1,180.
