What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice is a legal document issued by a registered business showing the details of goods or services sold, including the GST amount charged. Under GST law in India, every registered business must issue tax invoices for taxable supplies.

Key Components of a GST Invoice

Every GST invoice must include:

  • Invoice Number: Unique sequential number (e.g., INV-001)
  • Invoice Date: Date of supply
  • Supplier Details: Name, address, and GSTIN
  • Customer Details: Name, address, and GSTIN (if registered)
  • Item Details: Description, quantity, unit price, HSN/SAC code
  • Tax Breakdown: CGST, SGST, or IGST amounts
  • Total Amount: Grand total including taxes

Common GST Invoice Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Missing GSTIN: Always include valid GSTIN
  2. Wrong tax type: Use IGST for inter-state, CGST+SGST for intra-state
  3. Duplicate invoice numbers: Maintain unique sequential numbering
  4. Missing HSN/SAC codes: Required for items above certain value
  5. Incorrect calculations: Use automated tools to avoid errors
