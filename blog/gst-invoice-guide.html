What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice is a legal document issued by a registered business showing the details of goods or services sold, including the GST amount charged. Under GST law in India, every registered business must issue tax invoices for taxable supplies.

GST invoices serve multiple purposes: they are proof of sale, record of tax collected, and essential for claiming input tax credit (ITC). Both the seller and buyer must maintain invoice copies for GST compliance and filing returns.

Key Components of a GST Invoice

Every GST invoice must include the following mandatory fields:

  • Invoice Number: Unique sequential number (e.g., INV-001, INV-002)
  • Invoice Date: Date of supply of goods or services
  • Supplier Details: Business name, complete address, and 15-digit GSTIN
  • Customer Details: Name, billing address, and GSTIN (if registered)
  • Item Details: Description, quantity, unit of measurement, unit price
  • HSN/SAC Code: Harmonized System Nomenclature for goods or Service Accounting Code for services
  • Tax Breakdown: Separate CGST and SGST for intra-state or IGST for inter-state transactions
  • Total Amount: Grand total including all taxes payable by customer

Common GST Invoice Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these frequent errors that can lead to compliance issues or rejected invoices:

  1. Missing or Invalid GSTIN: Always include valid, active GSTIN for both parties in B2B transactions
  2. Wrong Tax Type: Use IGST for inter-state sales, CGST+SGST for intra-state transactions
  3. Duplicate Invoice Numbers: Maintain unique, sequential numbering system without gaps
  4. Missing HSN/SAC Codes: Mandatory for businesses above certain turnover thresholds
  5. Incorrect Calculations: Always use automated tools to prevent manual calculation errors
  6. Wrong Place of Supply: Incorrect state selection affects IGST vs CGST+SGST
  7. Missing Mandatory Fields: Include invoice date, due date, and total in words
  8. Wrong GST Rate: Verify applicable GST rate for each product/service

GST Invoice Rules 2026

Important GST regulations to remember for compliance:

  • Invoice Requirement: Mandatory for all taxable supplies above ₹200
  • Time Limit: Must be issued within 30 days of supply for goods, before/at time of service
  • Digital Validity: Electronic invoices in PDF format are legally valid and acceptable
  • E-Invoicing: Mandatory for businesses with annual turnover above ₹5 crore
  • Record Retention: Invoices must be preserved for 72 months (6 years) from due filing date
  • Invoice Number: Must be unique, sequential, and cannot be repeated in a financial year
  • Reverse Charge: Special invoice format required for reverse charge mechanism
  • Export Invoices: Different format for exports with "Supply meant for export" endorsement
  • Credit/Debit Notes: Required for any post-sale adjustments, not invoice modifications

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is online invoice legally valid in India?

A: Yes, digital invoices are legally valid in India as per GST rules. They must contain all mandatory fields including GSTIN, invoice number, date, itemized list with HSN/SAC codes, and tax breakup. Electronic invoices can be issued in PDF format and are accepted by GST authorities for all compliance purposes.

Q: Do I need to register for GST to issue invoices?

A: If your annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states), GST registration is mandatory. Below this threshold, you can issue bills without GST. However, once registered, you must include your GSTIN on all invoices for taxable supplies and file regular GST returns.

Q: Can I edit invoice after issuing it?

A: No, once an invoice is issued, it cannot be edited or modified. To correct errors, you must issue a credit note (for reduction in value) or debit note (for increase in value). For major changes, cancel the original invoice and issue a fresh invoice with revised details and a new sequential invoice number.

Q: How to calculate GST manually on invoice?

A: Formula: GST Amount = (Base Amount × GST Rate) ÷ 100. For example, 18% GST on ₹1,000: (1000 × 18) ÷ 100 = ₹180. Total amount = Base + GST = ₹1,000 + ₹180 = ₹1,180. For intra-state transactions: split equally as CGST and SGST (9% each). For inter-state: apply full rate as IGST (18%).

Q: What is HSN code and is it mandatory on invoices?

A: HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) code is an internationally recognized system for classifying goods. For businesses with turnover above ₹5 crore, 6-digit HSN code is mandatory. For turnover between ₹1.5-5 crore, 4-digit code is required. Below ₹1.5 crore, HSN is optional but recommended for clarity.
