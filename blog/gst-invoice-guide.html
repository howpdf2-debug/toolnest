What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice is a legal document issued by a registered business showing the details of goods or services sold, including the GST amount charged. Under GST law in India, every registered business must issue tax invoices for taxable supplies.

GST invoices serve multiple purposes: they are proof of sale, record of tax collected, and essential for claiming input tax credit (ITC). Both seller and buyer must maintain invoice copies for GST compliance.

Key Components of GST Invoice

  • Invoice Number: Unique sequential number (e.g., INV-001)
  • Invoice Date: Date of supply
  • Supplier Details: Name, address, and GSTIN
  • Customer Details: Name, address, and GSTIN (if registered)
  • Item Details: Description, quantity, unit price, HSN/SAC code
  • Tax Breakdown: CGST, SGST, or IGST amounts
  • Total Amount: Grand total including taxes

Common GST Invoice Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Missing GSTIN: Always include valid GSTIN
  2. Wrong tax type: Use IGST for inter-state, CGST+SGST for intra-state
  3. Duplicate invoice numbers: Maintain unique sequential numbering
  4. Missing HSN/SAC codes: Required for items above certain value
  5. Incorrect calculations: Use automated tools to avoid errors
  6. Wrong place of supply: Affects IGST vs CGST+SGST

GST Invoice Rules 2026

Important regulations to remember:

  • Mandatory: For all taxable supplies above ₹200
  • Time Limit: Must be issued within 30 days of supply
  • Digital Valid: Electronic invoices are legally valid
  • E-Invoicing: Mandatory for businesses above ₹5 crore turnover
  • Retention: Invoices must be preserved for 72 months

Frequently Asked Questions

Is online invoice legally valid in India?

Yes, digital invoices are legally valid in India as per GST rules. They must contain all mandatory fields including GSTIN, invoice number, date, itemized list with HSN/SAC codes, and tax breakup.

Do I need GST registration to issue invoices?

If your annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states), GST registration is mandatory. Below this threshold, you can issue bills without GST.

Can I edit invoice after issuing?

No, once an invoice is issued, it cannot be edited. To correct errors, you must issue a credit note or debit note. For major changes, cancel the original and issue a new invoice.

How to calculate GST manually?

Formula: GST Amount = (Base Amount × GST Rate) ÷ 100

Example: 18% GST on ₹1,000: (1000 × 18) ÷ 100 = ₹180. Total = ₹1,180
